Importance of Drama and Puppetry

Drama and puppetry are powerful tools in early childhood education, offering children a safe space to explore emotions, narratives, and social roles. According to Joseph (2022), puppetry and dramatic play enhance communication skills, empathy, and imagination, enabling children to embody and externalize their thoughts. These activities encourage active participation, group collaboration, and problem-solving, which are essential for holistic development. Puppets and role play give children opportunities to act out familiar or imagined scenarios, strengthening both cognitive flexibility and emotional regulation. As a result, drama and puppetry become integral components in developing creative confidence and social-emotional awareness in young learners.

Creativity Theories and Perspectives in Drama

Drama and puppetry in early childhood education are supported by both symbolic interactionism and constructivist theory. Children use symbolic play to express their understanding of the world and develop new meanings through interaction. Ocal et al. (2021) emphasize that puppetry supports inquiry-based learning, particularly in science education, by engaging children in hands-on discovery and storytelling. Furthermore, the imaginative engagement in pretend play aligns with Torrance‚Äôs theory of creativity, which highlights fluency, originality, and elaboration‚Äîskills fostered through dramatization and role-play.
